Maternal Line

The Nelson, Tucker, Miller, Bucher, Williamson, and Eiler side is heavily rooted in northern Indiana.

Confirmed

Jerry Nelson

1938–2021. Born in Warsaw, Indiana; lifelong Kosciusko County / Winona Lake resident. His obituary anchors the Nelson line and names parents Denzel Nelson and Lena Williamson Nelson.

Jerry farmed locally and owned/operated Nelson Beverage. His story centers around Warsaw, Winona Lake, Beaver Dam, and Burket.

Strong lead

Denzel / Denzil Nelson + Lena Williamson

Denzel/Denzil is confirmed as Jerry’s father, but his own 1977 obituary/death record is still missing. Lena is confirmed as Jerry’s mother; her likely death window is Jan.–Feb. 1939.

A strong but still indirect lead points to Masa G. Nelson + Mina Barkman Nelson as Denzel/Denzil’s parents.

Confirmed

Mary E. Miller Tucker Bucher

1916–2015. Born in Claypool, Indiana; died in Winona Lake; buried at Mentone Cemetery. Her obituary confirms parents Cloyce Miller and Roxie Parker Miller.

She married Edison C. Tucker first, then C. Edward Bucher. Her obituary directly links Mary → JoAnn → Tara.

Confirmed

Edison C. Tucker

1911–1969. Born in Mentone, Indiana; died in rural Mentone; buried at Mentone Cemetery. His obituary confirms parents Charles M. Tucker and Suzannah Eiler Tucker.

This opens the Tucker / Eiler branch for future research.

Paternal Line

Family confirmation and public records tie him into the Robert H. / Robert Huntington Carlile, Columbus Academy, and Princeton cluster.

The family connects to Columbus Academy, Princeton, Green Lawn Cemetery, W. S. Carlile Sons Co., and Walloon Lake summer history.

Confirmed

Allene “Lena” Turpin Carlile

1943–2021. Born in Richmond, Virginia; died in Grosse Pointe Park, Michigan. Her obituary names parents William G. Turpin and Lucille Andrews Turpin, and former husband Robert Carlile.

Her line opens into the Turpin / Andrews / Beazley cluster in Virginia.

The Carlile-Huntington Breakthrough

The Robert side originally had scattered clues: Princeton, Ohio, manufacturing, Huntington, Walloon Lake, and train/summer-house memories. Those clues converged around a Columbus, Ohio family: William Arthur Carlile + Ruth Huntington Carlile.

The strongest bridge came from William Harlow Carlile’s Princeton Alumni Weekly memorial, which says he followed his father William A. ’36, uncle John ’38, and brother Robert ’64 to Princeton. His obituary then identifies him as the youngest child of Mary and William A. Carlile Jr., with siblings Robert and Molly.

Why that mattered

It tied together almost every family-memory clue at once: Columbus Academy, Princeton, Ohio roots, manufacturing, Huntington as a family surname, and Walloon Lake summers.
